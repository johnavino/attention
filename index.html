A Field Guide
to Attention

A growing set of contemplative practices, grouped by what you actually do. Each card gives you the steps, what tends to happen, where it comes from, and where it can quietly go sideways — an invitation to explore with clear eyes.

Browse by what the practice is, or filter to one family above. Tap any card for the steps. Each practice names the tradition it grew from — worth knowing whose centuries of work you're drawing on, and where a practice can lead if you want to follow it further.

Practices don't map one-to-one to outcomes, but each tends to develop certain capacities more than others. Pick a quality to see the practices most associated with it.

Not "which practice is best" but "what do I do right now." Situational sets for specific moments. Tap a practice to jump to its card.

A two-week on-ramp. Short, in order, no equipment. The goal isn't to feel calm — it's to get familiar with paying attention on purpose and noticing when you've drifted. Drifting is not failure; noticing the drift is the rep.
